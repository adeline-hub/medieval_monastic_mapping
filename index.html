Beyond the 'Sacred' Walls: Mapping Power, Privilege, and People in Medieval Monasteries

This report dives deep into medieval monastic communities across Europe, middle East, and Africa. We're using a dataset we've painstakingly pulled together from historical records to unpick patterns in how they were spread out, who exactly made them up, and what their actual societal impact was, beyond the stained glass and hymns.

These monastic communities were a pretty big deal in medieval society, acting as key hubs for... well, 'spirituality' (if you're into that), alongside education and some seriously powerful economic activity. This project smashes together historical investigation with proper modern data science, using robust analysis techniques to get a structured, no-nonsense grip on these institutions.

We're taking centuries of medieval insight – specifically about which monastic communities held absolute exclusivity or near-monopolies on power – and then we're throwing in data science to blow open what was essentially a closed shop, keeping wealth and influence firmly in the hands of a select, often male, few.

Things like wealth, family ties, health, and secure employment were absolutely fundamental attributes of community life back in the medieval period.

Looking at the dataset we've meticulously compiled from various sources, it's crystal clear that the medieval period was a rich and intense time. Most of the communities we've gathered data on were founded between 500 and 1500 CE.

My objective is to ensure we're utterly fair to every country already cited, not just by the sheer number of communities we've pulled in, but crucially, by how gender is represented throughout. I'll be churning out regular updates, so watch this space.

The combined dataset was last updated in May 2025.

Introduction: Context, Methodology & The Unseen Narrative




Right then, so what's the real objective behind dissecting centuries of monastic life? Beyond the pious pronouncements and the glorified histories, the mission here is absolutely crucial: to peel back the layers of the long medieval period and, in particular, to unearth the often-silenced story of women within these supposedly 'sacred' power structures, where wealth and power were coded. Because if we don't properly grasp the deeply ingrained social and economic hierarchies that shaped lives then – particularly how gender, wealth, and perceived 'divine' authority intersected – how on earth can we truly understand the deep-seated defaults and inequalities plaguing our so-called 'democracies' today? Thisis about confronting historical power dynamics to illuminate our own.

To achieve this, we’re wielding the very best of modern data analysi techniques. Forget relying solely on vague chronicles penned by blokes with agendas; applying rigorous data science to a meticulously compiled dataset, transforming disparate historical records into a structured, quantifiable understanding. This methodological approach allows us to identify trends, outliers, and, crucially, the hidden patterns of privilege and exclusion that traditional narratives have conveniently overlooked for centuries.




Part 1: The Economic Clout of Monasteries




Let’s cut through the pious fog right from the start, shall we? Behind every whispered prayer and solemn chant in these medieval monasteries was often a very substantial landholding and a tidy profit to be had. These weren’t just houses of God; they were economic powerhouses, acting as major players in the medieval economy – think grand landowners, bustling centres of production, and shrewd managers of resources. While the sermons might’ve been about salvation, the ledgers told a different story, one of wealth accumulation, control, and, more often than not, the reinforcement of hierarchies that kept certain folks – especially women – on the margins.
